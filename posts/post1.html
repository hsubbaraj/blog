The Inspiration Behind This Blog

Posted by Hari Subbaraj on October 18, 2015

I have a notoriously short attention span. As my mother never fails to point out, I cannot think about or work on an activity for more than a few hours, whether it is academics or even something that interests me.

So why then, am I writing a blog? On the surface, it seems exactly like the activity that I hate: something that is time consuming and requires incredible focus. The idea came to me over the weekend, in three separate stages that culminated in a triumphant yet silent first pump (It was 2 in the morning, couldn’t wake up my parents) as I pushed this blog template onto Github Pages. So thank you to whomever is reading this, and please bear with me as I try to prove to myself that I am not a crappy writer.

Show and tell was my favorite game in elementary school. I loved listening to other people… eh who am I kidding; I loved “watch me show and tell”. I could bring anything that I wanted and show my friends, anything, as long as it wasn’t dangerous, inappropriate, religious, indicative of violence… I think you get the gist of this. To revise the previous statement, I could bring anything that I wanted to as long as I was allowed to bring it. So in essence, we were supposed to bring an object to class that told our classmates that we were normal kids with normal lives.

So maybe I am being harsh, after all, these are six year olds that we are talking about. So fast forward 11 years and here I am, writing essays to show (not tell, as Ms. Lindsey has drilled into my head) colleges who I am. But as I draft more and more essays, I realize that I am not showing these colleges who I am, but rather a carefully constructed, but undoubtedly fake version of me. I hope other people find this as ironic as I do: I am still playing the same game of show and tell that I was 11 years earlier.

So that’s what this blog is all about: to put my true self out there, without any censorship whatsoever, to express my opinions without fear of hate or rejection, to open myself up to the world. I truly believe that if I can handle this introspection, something I’ve never done before, I will learn more about myself and be content with who I am as a person. So lets begin show and tell, version 2: No holds barred (for those of you who aren’t into wrestling, it means no rules apply).

And on that note, I have a small confession to make: no matter how well you think you know me, you don’t. I mean that not in a hurtful manner, but as a fact. I have hid parts of myself from the people I know and love, and it is definitely time to stop doing that. Hopefully this blog is the first step in that direction. Sorry for being so serious in this post, I am usually not this emotional or deep. I just thought it would be nice if you as readers knew my motivations behind this blog and why I randomly started it. Thank you once again for reading this, and I’m very excited to see where this goes!!!

Last thing: if I could bring something for show and tell, it would be my fucking Common App essay, cause I really need to get that shit edited :O. Let me know what you folks would bring in the comments! Note: Comments not yet up, will be very soon
